title: "Step 4: The cohorts tab"
description: "How to use the Cohorts tab to complete assigned forms, sign reports, manage your learning contract, and enroll in cohorts."
---

The Cohorts tab organizes your academic work. It's the best place to see everything you still need to do — pending forms, reports awaiting your signature, and urgent items.

## Inside a cohort

Click a cohort card to open it. Inside you'll see your tasks organized as:

- **To Do** — tasks you haven't started
- **In Progress** — forms or reports you've partially completed
- **Completed** — submitted forms and signed reports

Most of your tasks here will be filling out hour logs or completing evaluation forms assigned by your faculty.

## Completing an assigned form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the form">
    On the Cohorts tab, look at the **Upcoming tasks** panel at the top. Forms appear as task cards.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Open">
    Click the task card or **Open** to launch the form in a new tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete and submit">
    Fill out the form and submit. If you need to edit a submitted form, look for a **Review / Resubmit** option on the form. If you don't see one, you may need to ask your coordinator to reset the assignment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Enrolling in a cohort

If your program gives you a cohort code:

1. Click **Enroll in cohort** in the page header
2. Enter the code your program provided
3. Submit to join

## Learning contract

If your program uses learning contracts, a **Learning Contract** card appears above the cohort grid. Click **Start** (or **Continue**) to fill it out, then click **Send for Sign-Off** to request your supervisor's signature.

## Finding supervision reports

All pending items — including reports awaiting your signature — are in the Cohorts tab's Upcoming tasks panel. You can also find reports under **My Dashboard > Profile Details > Timesheet Reports**.

<Note>The Cohorts tab only shows content after your clearance is approved. If the tab shows a blocked overlay, complete your clearance tasks first.</Note>
