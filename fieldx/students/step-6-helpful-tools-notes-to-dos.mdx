At the top of your dashboard you'll find two tools to help stay organized throughout your placement, plus an AI-generated activity summary to help you reflect on your progress.

## To-dos

Click the **To-Do** button at the top of your dashboard to create reminders and tasks.

There are two ways to use To-Dos:

- **Personal reminders** — create a task for yourself (e.g., "Upload insurance renewal by Friday", "Submit weekly log before 5pm")
- **Remind others** — create a task to remind a supervisor or peer about an upcoming deadline (e.g., "Supervisor needs to sign off on last week's report")

<Tip>
To-dos are lightweight and don't require any approval or coordination — use them freely to track anything that isn't a formal assignment or logged hour. They're especially useful when you're juggling multiple cohorts or deadlines at once.
</Tip>

## My notes (journaling)

Click the **My Notes** button to access the journaling feature. Notes are a space for reflection, documentation, and communication with your faculty. You can create three types of entries:

| Type | Who can see it | Best used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Private** | Only you | Personal reflections, processing difficult experiences, draft thoughts |
| **Public** | You + your faculty | Sharing your development, demonstrating growth, faculty check-ins |
| **Supervision notes** | You + your supervisor + faculty | Documenting specific supervision sessions and discussion points |

<Note>
Writing regular notes — even short ones — builds a record of your professional development over the term. Private notes help you process experiences in the moment. Public notes give your faculty visibility into your growth without requiring a formal meeting.
</Note>

<Warning>
Supervision notes are not a substitute for a formal supervision report. They document the conversation, but hours logged under the Supervision hour type still need to be tied to a submitted supervision report.
</Warning>

## AI activity summary

On **My Dashboard**, switch to the **Activity** segment (the two-tab toggle near the top of the page). There you'll find:

- A **donut chart** breaking down your logged hours by type (Direct, Indirect, Supervision)
- An **AI-generated summary** of your activity for the selected period

The summary generates automatically once you have logged hours. If you've added new hours since the last summary was generated, click **Refresh** to regenerate it with the latest data.

<Tip>
Use the AI summary when writing reflection papers, preparing for supervision sessions, or reviewing your progress mid-term. It gives you a quick narrative of what you've been doing without having to dig through individual timesheet rows.
</Tip>

## Common issues

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| **To-Do button doesn't appear** | Make sure you're on your main dashboard. The To-Do and My Notes buttons are in the top toolbar — try scrolling up if you don't see them. |
| **I can't find a note I created** | Notes are filtered by type. Check that you haven't filtered to a specific type that excludes the note you're looking for. Private notes are only visible to you — they won't appear in any faculty-facing views. |
| **The AI activity summary is blank** | The summary only generates once you have approved or unapproved hours logged. If your Timesheet is empty, log your first hours and then return to the Activity tab. |
| **The AI summary looks outdated** | Click the **Refresh** button on the Activity segment. It will regenerate the summary based on your current logged hours for the selected period. |
| **I see "No activity" even though I've logged hours** | Check the period selector on the Activity segment — it may be scoped to a different term or date range than the hours you logged. Switch the period to match where your hours are. |
| **Supervision notes aren't showing up for my supervisor** | Supervision notes are linked to your supervisor via your cohort. If your supervisor hasn't been added to your cohort yet, ask your coordinator to confirm the connection. |
