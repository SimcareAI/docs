The Advanced Skills report breaks down specific clinical communication techniques you used — how often, how well, and with concrete examples pulled directly from your session.

## Skills the AI evaluates

### Core communication skills

- **Active listening** — demonstrating attention and understanding through verbal and behavioral cues
- **Empathy** — recognizing and validating the patient's emotions
- **Open-ended questions** — encouraging detailed responses rather than yes/no answers
- **Reflective statements** — mirroring the patient's thoughts and feelings back to them
- **Summarization** — condensing information effectively to check understanding

### Clinical assessment skills

- **History taking** — systematic gathering of background information
- **Symptom exploration** — thorough investigation of the patient's presenting complaints
- **Risk assessment** — identifying safety concerns (suicidality, self-harm, danger to others)
- **Differential diagnosis** — considering multiple possible explanations

### Therapeutic techniques

- **Motivational interviewing** — enhancing readiness for change through OARS (open questions, affirmations, reflections, summaries)
- **Psychoeducation** — teaching effectively about conditions or treatment
- **Goal setting** — collaborative planning with the patient
- **Problem solving** — structured approach to challenges

## Understanding your skills report

### Skill ratings

| Rating | Score | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Excellent | 90–100 | Demonstrated consistently and effectively |
| Good | 70–89 | Used appropriately with minor refinements possible |
| Developing | 50–69 | Emerging but needs more deliberate practice |
| Needs work | Below 50 | Rarely demonstrated or ineffectively applied |

### What's included for each skill

- **Frequency** — how many times you used the technique
- **Quality rating** — how effectively you applied it
- **Examples** — specific transcript moments linked to the annotation
- **Improvement suggestions** — concrete next steps

## Common skills to develop

### Open-ended questions

Closed questions cut off exploration:
> "Are you feeling sad?" → **Try instead:** "How have you been feeling emotionally?"

Avoid multiple questions at once:
> "Are you sleeping okay and eating well and do you have support?" → **Try instead:** Ask one, wait, then follow up.

### Empathic responses

Dismissive responses move on too quickly:
> "That's normal, don't worry about it." → **Try instead:** "It sounds like this has been really difficult for you."

### Reflective listening

Moving on without reflecting misses the opportunity to validate:
> "Okay, what happened next?" → **Try instead:** "So what I'm hearing is that you felt overwhelmed when that happened."

## How to use the skills report

1. Identify your 2–3 lowest-rated skills
2. Review the specific transcript examples for those skills
3. Read the improvement suggestions
4. In your next session, practice those skills deliberately — tell yourself before you start: "I'm going to use more reflective statements today"
5. Track how your ratings change across sessions

<Note>Skill ratings consider context: appropriateness of timing, the patient's needs, and the clinical phase (assessment vs. intervention). A technique that's excellent in one moment may not be appropriate in another. If you disagree with a rating, use [Chat with Feedback](/simulations/chat-with-feedback) to understand the AI's reasoning.</Note>
