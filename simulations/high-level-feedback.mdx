The High-Level Feedback is the first report you'll see after a session. It gives you an overall score (0–100) and scores across four competency categories, plus a written executive summary of your session.

## Overall performance score

Your overall score is a composite rating based on:

- Communication effectiveness
- Clinical assessment thoroughness
- Therapeutic skills application
- Professional demeanor
- Scenario-specific objectives

### Score tiers

| Score | Level | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 90–100 | Excellent | Advanced clinical communication demonstrated consistently throughout the session |
| 75–89 | Proficient | Solid performance with effective use of core skills and minor areas for refinement |
| 60–74 | Developing | Basic competency demonstrated but significant room for skill development |
| Below 60 | Needs improvement | Foundational skills need strengthening before progressing to more complex scenarios |

<Note>Score decreases between sessions are normal — harder scenarios, trying new techniques, or simply a different patient dynamic can all lower scores temporarily. Compare yourself to your own baseline, not others.</Note>

## Competency categories

Your score breaks down into four areas, each rated 0–100:

### 1. Communication skills

Evaluates:
- Clarity of expression
- Active listening
- Question formulation (open vs. closed)
- Verbal empathy

### 2. Clinical assessment

Evaluates:
- History-taking completeness
- Symptom exploration
- Risk assessment
- Information organization

### 3. Therapeutic relationship

Evaluates:
- Rapport building
- Trust establishment
- Patient-centered approach
- Cultural sensitivity

### 4. Professional skills

Evaluates:
- Session structure
- Time management
- Appropriate boundaries
- Ethical considerations

## Executive summary

Below the scores, a written narrative highlights:

- **Top 3 strengths** — what you did particularly well
- **Top 3 areas for growth** — priority improvement areas
- **Overall approach** — your general clinical style
- **Key takeaways** — main learning points from this session

## How to use high-level feedback

<Steps>
  <Step title="Note your overall score">
    This gives context for the detailed feedback that follows. Is it in the tier you expected?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify your strongest category">
    Understanding your natural clinical strengths helps you build confidence and leverage them in weaker areas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Focus on your lowest-scoring category">
    Your lowest area should be the primary focus for your next session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the executive summary">
    The narrative explains *why* certain scores are high or low. It highlights patterns and specific moments that influenced your ratings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drill down into other reports">
    - Low communication score → [Advanced Skills](/simulations/advanced-skills)
    - Want specific line-by-line examples → [Annotated Transcript](/simulations/annotated-transcript)
    - Curious about theoretical approach → [Theory Analysis](/simulations/theory-analysis)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Setting goals based on feedback

Use your high-level feedback to create focused, actionable practice goals:

- **Specific:** "Improve my empathy score from 65 to 75"
- **Measurable:** Track score changes across sessions on your dashboard
- **Achievable:** Focus on 1–2 categories at a time, not everything at once
- **Time-bound:** "Within the next 5 practice sessions"

A good rule of thumb: spend 70% of your practice on weak areas and 30% maintaining and expanding your strengths.

<Tip>Review your high-level feedback first for the big picture, then dive into the [Annotated Transcript](/simulations/annotated-transcript) for specific examples, and use [Chat with Feedback](/simulations/chat-with-feedback) to ask follow-up questions about anything you don't understand.</Tip>
