If the AI patient can't hear you, or you're seeing microphone-related errors, here's how to fix it.

## Step 1: Unmute yourself

First, make sure you've unmuted your microphone in the session. Look for the microphone icon in your session controls and click it to unmute.

![Microphone unmute icon in session controls](/images/mic_1.webp)

## Step 2: Fix browser permissions

The most common cause of microphone problems on a first-time session is accidentally blocking browser permissions.

When Chrome or Edge first asks for microphone access, if you clicked **"Block"** by mistake:

1. Click the **lock icon** in your browser address bar (top left)
2. Set microphone access to **Allow**
3. Set camera access to **Allow** (if applicable)

![Browser permissions settings showing lock icon](/images/mic_2.webp)

After updating permissions, go back to the Discover page and start the session again.

## Step 3: Check your mic settings on the setup screen

When you reach the session setup screen, you'll see an audio check. Speak into your mic and watch the input meter — if it moves when you speak, your mic is working. If it doesn't move at all, your browser may still not have access.

<Warning>
**Supported browsers:** Chrome and Microsoft Edge only. Safari and mobile browsers are not supported. If you're on an unsupported browser, switch to Chrome first before troubleshooting anything else.
</Warning>

## Still having issues?

- Try using an Incognito/Private window — this rules out browser extension conflicts
- If you have multiple microphones (e.g., headset + built-in), confirm the right one is selected in the setup screen dropdown
- Restart your browser entirely
