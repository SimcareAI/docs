title: "Chat with feedback"

Chat with Feedback is an AI tutor that knows your full session — the transcript, the scores, every annotation. Ask it questions in plain language and it explains, demonstrates, and helps you understand exactly how to improve.

## What you can ask

### About specific moments

- "Why was my question at 5:32 highlighted yellow?"
- "What would have been a better response when the patient mentioned feeling anxious?"
- "Can you explain why my closing statement was rated positively?"

### About skills and techniques

- "How can I improve my use of open-ended questions?"
- "What are examples of good empathic responses for this patient?"
- "How do I balance gathering information with building rapport?"

### About overall performance

- "What were my top 3 strengths in this session?"
- "What should I focus on improving next time?"
- "Create a practice plan based on this feedback"

### About clinical approaches

- "How does motivational interviewing apply to this scenario?"
- "How could I have explored the biopsychosocial factors more effectively?"
- "What would a more person-centered approach have looked like?"

## Getting the most from Chat with Feedback

### Be specific

Vague questions get generic answers. Reference your actual session:

> "My empathy score was 65. What specific techniques could I use to demonstrate more empathy in mental health scenarios?"

is more useful than:

> "How can I do better?"

### Reference the transcript

Ask about specific exchanges:

- "Analyze the exchange around the 3:45 mark"
- "What was wrong with my first question?"
- "Walk me through why the middle of the session scored lower"

### Request examples

Ask for concrete demonstrations:

- "Give me 3 examples of better ways I could have asked that"
- "Show me what a good summary would look like for this patient"
- "What would an expert clinician say in that moment?"

### Explore what-ifs

- "What if the patient had been more resistant?"
- "How would this approach differ for an older adult patient?"
- "What if I had taken a more CBT-focused approach?"

## Common use cases

**Understanding a low score** — Ask why it was low, get specific examples from your session, request improvement recommendations.

**Building on strengths** — "My reflective listening was rated highly. How can I apply this strength in more challenging situations?"

**Preparing for supervision** — Use the AI to articulate what happened and why, then bring those insights into your supervision session.

**Preparing for your next session** — "What 3 things should I focus on in my next practice session based on this feedback?"

<Tip>Use Chat with Feedback as a conversation starter with your supervisor — not a replacement for human supervision. Ask the AI for perspectives and practice articulating them, then discuss with your supervisor for deeper clinical insight.</Tip>

<Note>Chat with Feedback cannot change or re-score your feedback. It provides AI-generated guidance focused on communication skills, not medical diagnosis. For complex clinical judgment questions, human supervision remains essential.</Note>
