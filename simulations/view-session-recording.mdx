Every SimCare session is recorded and available for playback. Listening to yourself is one of the most powerful learning tools available — it reveals things about your tone, pace, and verbal habits that are hard to notice in the moment.

## Accessing your recording

Find your recording in:
- The session feedback dashboard (after feedback loads)
- Your session history / Library page
- The feedback notification email

Recordings are available for **6 months** after your session.

## Playback controls

- **Play/Pause** — standard controls
- **Speed** — 0.75× to 2× playback speed
- **Skip** — 10-second forward/back jumps
- **Timeline markers** — timestamps at key moments flagged in your feedback
- **Download** — save as MP3 for offline review

## What to listen for

### Your vocal qualities

Hearing yourself reveals things the transcript can't capture:
- Tone (warm, clinical, flat, anxious)
- Pace (are you rushing? leaving space for the patient?)
- Filler words ("um," "like," "you know")
- Moments of hesitation or confidence

### Follow along with the transcript

Play the recording while reading your [Annotated Transcript](/simulations/annotated-transcript). Hearing the context behind highlighted moments helps you understand *why* a statement landed well or poorly. Tone matters — the exact same words can read as empathic or dismissive depending on delivery.

### Compare across sessions

Listen to recordings from different sessions to:
- Track vocal confidence over time
- Notice improvement in conversational flow
- Identify persistent speech patterns to work on
- Celebrate growth

## Learning activities

**Pause and predict** — Play a portion, pause before your next response, consider what you'd say differently now, then resume and compare.

**Vocal analysis** — Listen specifically for tone and emotion. Note where you sound empathic vs. robotic. Identify moments where your tone didn't match your intent.

**Supervision prep** — Identify 2–3 specific moments to bring to your supervisor. Share the relevant timestamp and prepare a question about it.

<Tip>Use headphones to catch subtle vocal nuances. Don't be too hard on yourself — hearing your own recorded voice is uncomfortable for almost everyone at first. Focus on one aspect per listen (empathy, then pacing, then question types) rather than trying to notice everything at once.</Tip>

<Note>Recordings are securely encrypted. Only you and authorized program administrators can access them. They are automatically deleted after the retention period (typically 6 months), or you can delete them earlier from your session history.</Note>
